About the Workshop

Overview

Personalization is being rebuilt around generative models. Large language models, retrieval-augmented generation, and agentic systems now shape how recommenders retrieve, rank, explain, converse, and even simulate users — yet they also surface hard problems of hallucination, control, evaluation, privacy, and fairness.

GRAIL 2026 brings together academic researchers and industry practitioners to advance generative, retrieval-augmented, and agentic approaches to recommendation, while giving equal attention to these open challenges and their long-term user impact.

The workshop is dedicated to generative recommender systems — methods that leverage generative models for ranking, retrieval, planning, and explanation, as well as for conversational agents, user simulation, and evaluation.

This theme is intrinsically tied to the core disciplines of CIKM: information retrieval, knowledge management, and machine learning. GRAIL seeks to establish a rigorous research agenda for generative personalization and a high-impact exchange between academia and industry on responsible deployment and evaluation.

Call for Papers

We invite contributions

GRAIL 2026 welcomes original research, position pieces, and practical contributions at the intersection of generative AI and personalization. Topics include, but are not limited to:

LLM-based recommendation Retrieval-augmented personalization Generative sequential & session-based recommendation Multimodal & content-generating recommenders Conversational & agentic recommendation Knowledge-grounded recommendation Synthetic interaction data User modeling with GenAI Evaluation of generated recommendations Fairness, privacy & safety Controllability & user feedback Scalable production architectures

Full research papers

up to 8 pages

Original contributions (excluding references).

Short & position papers

4–6 pages

Visionary or preliminary work (excluding references).

Demo, dataset & benchmark

up to 4 pages

Practical and evaluative contributions (excluding references).

All submissions follow the standard ACM double-column template (sigconf). Each paper receives at least three reviews. Papers are non-archival, so authors remain free to submit revised versions elsewhere. At least one author of each accepted paper must register and present in person in Rome.
